MOTHER TERESA

Early Life and Family

  • 1) Born on August 26, 1910, in Skopje, the current capital of the Republic of Macedonia, Mother Teresa was baptized the following day as Agnes Gonxha Bojaxhui.
  • 2) Her parents, Nikola and Dranafile Bojaxhui were of Albanian Descent. Her father worked as a construction contractor and a trader of medicines.
  • 3) Nikola was a vocal proponent involved in the local church as well as in city politics.
  • 4) In 1919, her father died due to unknown reasons while there were speculations that he was poisoned by political enemies.
  • 5) Later, Agnes became attached to her mother who instilled in her a deep commitment to charity.

Education

  • 1) Agnes went to a convent-run primary school and then a state-run secondary school.
  • 2) She used to sing solos in the local Sacred Heart choir and from there she first felt a calling to religious life at the age of 12.
  • 3) Six years later, in 1928, an 18-year-old Agnes decided to become a nun and set off for Ireland to join the sisters of Loreto in Dublin.
  • 4) It was there that she took the name Sister Mary Teresa after Saint Theresa of Liseux.

Nunhood

  • 1) A year later, Sister Mary Teresa travelled to Darjeeling, India for the Novitiate Period. In May 1931, She made her first profession of vows.
  • 2) Later, She went to Calcutta, where she was assigned to teach at Saint Mary’s High School for Girls, a school run by the Loreto sisters and dedicated to teaching girls from the city’s poorest Bengali families.
  • 3) On May 24, 1937, She took her final profession of vows to a life of poverty, chastity, and obedience.
  • 4) Later, she took on the title “Mother” upon making her final vows and became Mother Teresa. She continued her teaching and lead them to a life of devotion to Christ.

Missionaries of Charity

  • 1) Mother Teresa translated her calling into concrete actions to help the city’s poor. She began an open-air career and established a home for the dying institute.
  • 2) In 1950, she won canonical recognition for a new congregation, The Missionaries of Charity. Mother Teresa’s charitable activities expanded exponentially.
  • 3) Over the course of the 1950s and 1960s, she established a leper colony, an orphanage, a nursing home, a family clinic, and a string of mobile health clinics.
  • 4) In 1971, opened her American-based House of Charity.
  • 5) In 1985, Mother Teresa opened a home to care for those infected with HIV/AIDS in New York, United States.

Awards & Recognition

  • Mother Teresa received various honors for her tireless and effective Charity.
  • 1) In 1962, Mother Teresa was awarded the Padma Shri
  • 2) In 1969, she was awarded Jawaharlal Nehru Award for International Understanding
  • 3) In 1980, she was awarded Bharat Ratna
  • 4) In 1962, she received Ramon Magsaysay Award for Peace & International Understanding
  • 5) In 1971, she received Pope John XXII Peace Prize
  • 6) In 1973, she received Templeton Prize and in 1976, Pacem in Terris Award
  • 7) In 1982, she was appointed as an honorary Companion of the order of Australia

Death

  • After several years of deteriorating health, including heart, lung, and kidney problems, Mother Teresa died on September 5, 1997.

Canonization

  • 1) On December 17, 2015, Pope Francis issued an official order that recognized a second miracle attributed to Mother Teresa.
  • 2) She was canonized as a Saint on September 4, 2016, a day before the 19th anniversary of her death.
  • 3) Her canonization was held in St. Peter’s square in Vatican city. Thousands of Catholics and Pilgrims from around the world attended the canonization to celebrate the woman who had been called “the saint of the gutters” because she served the poor all her life.
